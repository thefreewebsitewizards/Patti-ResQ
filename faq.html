Frequently Asked Questions

Simple answers to the most common questions about the ResQ journey.

How is this different from obedience training?

expand_more

ResQ is not an obedience program. We don’t start with commands, corrections, or performance. We start with the human. Most adoption struggles happen because the dog is overwhelmed, not disobedient. ResQ focuses on preparation, emotional regulation, and understanding canine communication. When the human becomes calm, consistent, and clear, many behavioral issues resolve naturally. Obedience can come later. Trust must come first.

Do I need prior experience with dogs?

expand_more

No experience is required. In fact, many first-time adopters benefit the most from this approach. ResQ is designed to guide you step by step, helping you understand what rescue dogs need, how to read their signals, and how to prepare your home and energy before they ever arrive. The course meets you where you are, whether you’re brand new or simply ready to do things differently.

Is this course for adopters, fosters, or both?

expand_more

Both. ResQ is designed for: "First-time adopter guardians", "Returning dog guardians", "Foster family guardians". Anyone preparing to welcome a rescue dog into their home. The principles of calm leadership, patience, and understanding apply to any human caring for a dog in transition.

When should I take this course, before or after adopting?

expand_more

Ideally, before. ResQ was designed to prepare the human first, so the dog arrives into a calm, structured, and understanding environment. This dramatically increases the chances of a successful adoption. However, if you’ve already adopted and things feel stressful or confusing, this course can help you reset the relationship and rebuild trust.

How long is the course?

expand_more

The ResQ course is a four-session digital program that you can move through at your own pace. You’ll also have access to weekly live Zoom Q&A sessions for four weeks, where you can ask questions, share experiences, and receive guidance in real time. This structure allows you to learn the material on your schedule while still receiving personal support.

Is the program remote or in person?

expand_more

The program is fully remote. All course sessions are delivered online, and the live Q&A calls take place via Zoom. This allows you to participate from anywhere and learn in the environment where your dog will actually live—your home.

What if I’ve already adopted and things are difficult?

expand_more

You’re not alone. This is one of the most common situations people face. Many adopters bring a dog home with love and good intentions, only to encounter fear, reactivity, shutdown, or confusion. This doesn’t mean you chose the wrong dog or that you’ve failed. It simply means the dog is overwhelmed and needs time, structure, and understanding. ResQ helps you slow things down, reset expectations, and begin building trust in a calm, consistent way. Many relationships transform when the human changes first.

Will I get support after the course?

expand_more

Yes. In addition to the course sessions, you’ll have access to a private online community where you can connect with others on the same journey, ask questions, and share progress. You’ll also have the opportunity to join live Q&A sessions during the course period, and for those who want more individualized guidance, limited one-on-one support options may be available. The goal of ResQ is not just information, but ongoing support as you and your dog begin building trust together.

Still have a question?

We are here to help you on your journey to find your new best friend. Our team is ready to answer any specific inquiries you may have.

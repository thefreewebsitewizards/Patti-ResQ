The Philosophy

Why Train the
Human First?

We focus on preparation over pressure. The chaotic energy of a new home can be overwhelming for a rescue dog. By calming the human first, we create a sanctuary of safety.

Building a bond takes time, understanding, and the right mindset before we ever worry about "sit" or "stay". It's about learning the language of silence and trust.

Guidance for Life’s
In-Between Seasons

I’m Patti L. Curtin, the founder of Journey in Twilight—a place for education, reflection, and practical support during life’s in-between seasons. Journey in Twilight was created to hold space for moments that require patience, understanding, and compassionate guidance rather than quick answers.

One of the most meaningful expressions of that work is ResQ a Dog: Save a Life, Training the Human. I created ResQ after years of working alongside rescue dogs and the humans who love them, where I repeatedly saw well-intentioned adoptions struggle, not because of lack of love, but because of lack of preparation and understanding.

ResQ focuses on slowing the adoption process down and training the human first. I help people honestly assess their lifestyle, expectations, and capacity for care, while learning how to recognize a dog’s needs beyond first impressions. This approach creates safer adoptions, stronger bonds, and better outcomes for both dogs and humans.

My work is rooted in patience, trust-building, and the belief that when education is delivered with compassion, rescue dogs are given their best chance at a stable, loving home - and humans are given the confidence to succeed.

Patti L. Curtin

Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding our unique approach to rehabilitation.

How is this different from obedience training? expand_more
Obedience training focuses on commands. We focus on state of mind. A dog that can "sit" but is anxious isn't truly rehabilitated. We address the root emotions first.
Do I need prior experience with dogs? expand_more
Not at all. In fact, sometimes having no prior "training" habits is better. We look for open hearts and patience above technical skill.
How long is the waitlist? expand_more
We accept new cohorts every quarter. This allows us to give dedicated attention to each human-dog pair. The current wait time is approximately 4-6 weeks.
Is this program remote or in-person? expand_more
We offer a hybrid model. The "Train the Human" theoretical modules are online, while the practical bonding sessions are in-person at our sanctuary or your home.
